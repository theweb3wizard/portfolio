I've been building end-to-end with AI as my engineering wingman. Three things I've learned shipping this way:

**1. Architecture is still the bottleneck.**
AI can write 10,000 lines of code. It cannot decide your data model, your state strategy, or how your system fails gracefully. That's still a human decision. The better your architecture, the better the output.

**2. Polish is a differentiator.**
Anyone can scaffold a CRUD app. A responsive layout with Three.js, ambient color transitions, device-adaptive rendering, and JSON-LD structured data — that takes taste + iteration. AI accelerates both, but you have to know what good looks like.

**3. Speed without quality is noise.**
Shipping fast means nothing if the thing breaks. Tests pass. CI runs. TypeScript strict. Reduced-motion respected. Working fast means working clean.
